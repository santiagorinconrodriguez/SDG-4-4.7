Data Analysis and Interpretation of Survey Results

  1. Oxfam highlights the importance of being able to “find, interpret, present and manipulate data measuring progress towards the SDGs.”

    Once the survey is completed, an online data analysis exercise was made to interpret the findings and identify trends in students’ SDG knowledge. This includes graphs, statistics and conclusions. The survey itself helps participants to start questioning their knowledge and assumptions around SDGs.
survey results




ANALYSIS FROM GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT THE PARTICIPANTS

Within the results obtained in the survey, there are 5 main categories regarding the general information of the participants: age, faculty, the semester they are in, their gender, and finally whether they have had any previous training on the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Within the age category, an average age of approximately 20 years was obtained and a deviation of around 4.7 years. Most of the people interviewed are between 19 and 22 years old; the reason for a high standard deviation was that one person of 52 years old was interviewed, which considerably affects the statistical results. Nevertheless, these collected data allow us to identify that the vast majority of participants meet the objective age criterion to be considered young students of the National University...
